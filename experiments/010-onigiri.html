Notes

The triangular convenience-store onigiri sold across Japan hides a small marvel of packaging design. A sheet of nori is held away from the rice by a layer of film, so the seaweed stays crisp on the shelf instead of going soft against the wet rice. To eat it you run a three-step sequence printed right on the wrapper: pull tab ① to slit the film down the back, then ② and ③ to draw out each wing — and the nori folds onto the rice exactly as the plastic leaves.

A sandbox for that one gesture. Drag the numbered tabs in order, or click them; release past halfway and the pull completes, otherwise it springs back. Open it and a filling is revealed — umeboshi, sake, mentaiko, whatever the seed dealt. No goal beyond the satisfying unwrap. Your count is kept locally between visits.
