009 Particle Ocean

Notes

A field of points adrift on a slow current. Each one watches its neighbours: when two stray close enough, a hairline link is drawn between them; drift apart and it snaps. With no one watching, the field settles into a quietly shifting mesh — a plexus that is never quite the same twice.

Your cursor is a stone in the water. Move it through the field and the points nearby scatter (or gather, or spin), tearing the established web apart while the pointer itself reaches out in coral to whatever it can still touch. Press to throw a harder pulse. Switch the disturbance between Repel, Attract and Swirl; Calm stills the current, Reseed starts a fresh ocean.
