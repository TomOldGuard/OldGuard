011 Ronnie The Bear

Notes

A bear is a strange instrument. The roar that carries through a forest isn't a clean tone — it's a low fundamental dragged through a rough, half-broken throat: subharmonics, amplitude flutter, the grain of air forced past a closing larynx. Ronnie is an attempt to synthesise exactly that, live in the browser, with no samples. Two detuned sawtooth oscillators sit an octave apart for chest; a fast sawtooth LFO chews the amplitude into a growl; a waveshaper adds the throat's distortion; a band of filtered noise rides on top for breath. The lowpass filter swings open on the attack like a mouth, then closes on release.

Everything is one gesture. Drag across Ronnie — left to right sets pitch, up makes him fiercer — and hold to sustain the growl, following your hand. Let go and he settles. The sliders mirror the same two parameters, the presets jump to a mood, and an analyser reads the live signal back into his face: the mouth opens with the growl, the brow drops with grit, the ears flatten, and sound rings fan out when he really lets one go.
